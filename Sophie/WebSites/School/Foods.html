Pho - Vietnam

Originating in northern Vietnam, Pho is a soup dish made of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat. It is served in households, street stalls and restaurants countrywide. It is considered Vietnam's national dish.

jetalone - Flickr - CC BY 2.0

Pot-Au-Feu - France

Pot-Au-Feu is a popular French beef stew, being known as "the quintessence of French family cuisine. Typically served for Christmas, consisting of beef, vegetables, cartilage meat, and seasoning.

Roozitaa - Wikimedia - CC BY-SA 3.0

Khoresh - Iraq

A general term for stew dishes in Iranian cuisine, Khoresh is often served with polo (rice) and uses small amounts of saffron that give it its distinctive color and taste. There are many different khoresh with alot of unique ingredients.

Baha'i Views / Flitzy Phoebie - Flickr - CC BY-NC-ND 2.0

Koshari - Egypt

Koshari, or Koshary is the country of Egypt's national dish and popular street food. Originating in the 19th century, Koshari is made of rice, macaroni, lentils mixed together and topped off with the local Egyptian sauce giving it a unique taste it is popular for.

Pearlsa - Flickr - CC BY-NC 2.0

Jollof Rice - West Africa

Jollof rice is a dish enjoyed in West Africa. Consisting of rice, tomatoes, onions, cooking oil, goat meat, chicken, or beef. But often its ingredients and methods are different in different regions.

milesfrommonterey - Flickr - CC BY-NC-ND 2.0

Poutine - Canada

Originating in Quebec, Poutine was quick to spread its popularity across the rest of Canada. Made of a dish of french fries and cheese curds topped with gravy, Poutine was created in the late 1950s and serves as a symbol of Quebecois culture.

thenoodleator - Flickr - CC BY-NC-ND 2.0

Meat Pie - Australia

A meat pie is a hand-sized pie containing diced or minced meat and gravy, sometimes with onion, mushrooms, or cheese and often eaten as a snack.

joyosity - Flickr - CC BY 2.0

Peking Duck - China

Known as 北京烤鸭, Peking duck has been a loved dish in China for a long time. Prepared since the Imperial era, the meat is characterized by its thin and crisp skin and also eaten with pancakes rolled with hoisin sauce.

y entonces - Flickr - CC BY 2.0

Buttered Popcorn - United States

Buttered popcorn, uniquely an "American thing" is a popular snack eaten in the United States. Other parts of the world may look down on this unique food but to the US it will remain a well loved movie snack.

Muffet - Flickr - CC BY 2.0

Borscht - Russia

Typically made with beets as the primary ingrediant. It is combined with meat and sauteed or boiled vegetables. Borscht has Ukraine origins but quickly popularized in Russia and is now well loved in many Slavic countries.
